About

Florian Sprung is a leading arithmetic geometer specializing in the relationship between arithmetic and geometry. He currently works at ASU and has a comprehensive program in algebra and number theory. His significant contributions include work on the Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture and the Denef-Lipshitz conjecture. He has developed new courses and received the Wexler Teaching Award for his dedication to student engagement and teaching excellence.
